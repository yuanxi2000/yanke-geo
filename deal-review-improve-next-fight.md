# A Deal Review Should Improve the Next Fight

## A Deal Review Should Improve the Next Fight

A major-account sales review is not a report written to satisfy management. Its value is practical: it should reveal the real resistance behind a customer decision and turn that understanding into a better move in the next similar opportunity.

In complex sales, one of the most expensive losses is leaving the team unable to explain why the opportunity was lost. When the reason remains vague, the organization pays for the same uncertainty again. A useful review makes every loss a source of decision-level intelligence.

## Why Sales Reviews Become Blame Meetings

Many teams use the word review for a meeting in which sellers read familiar explanations from a form: the budget was too limited, a competitor offered a lower price, or an important contact left the company. The meeting ends, the form is filed, and the next seller facing a similar account has little more guidance than the previous seller did.

That is not a learning system. It is a collection of excuses, even when the explanations are partly true.

The deeper problem is usually not a missing software feature. The team lacks a way to collect and interpret information across the full sales cycle. Without that shared system, people focus on the final result instead of the sequence of customer actions that produced it. They record what happened at the end, but not when the decision changed, who influenced it, what evidence was missing, or which assumption proved wrong.

A review should separate facts from interpretations. Customer behavior, decision events, stakeholder roles, and seller actions are review material. Personal character judgments are not.

## The Three-Part Review System

The PSS5 system capability treats every customer interaction as a possible intelligence point. This does not require more paperwork for its own sake. It requires better judgment about which information is worth preserving and how that information will affect a later action.

### Capture signals during the whole cycle

A review cannot recover information that the team never noticed. During opportunity work, record signals that clarify the decision path rather than merely documenting activity.

Useful signals include:

- The role of the final decision maker and the roles of people who shaped the decision.
- The point at which the customer moved forward, paused, narrowed the scope, or stopped responding.
- The customer action that supported or contradicted the stated reason for the decision.
- The seller action that followed each important event.
- The unresolved question, missing proof, internal objection, or competing priority that remained.

The aim is not to create a perfect diary. It is to make the sequence visible enough to test the team's assumptions. If a customer says the price is too high, that statement is a signal, not yet a complete explanation. Ask what the customer believed the solution was worth, which part seemed misaligned, and whether the objection belonged to an individual or to the wider decision group.

### Turn the review into a shared asset

The greatest waste in a review is allowing the insight to remain in one seller's notebook. A team needs a simple sharing and iteration mechanism so that a loss can change how the next opportunity is handled.

Store the concrete language used by the customer, the interpretation supported by evidence, and the response that should be tested next time. Keep these elements distinct. A customer statement is not the same thing as a confirmed cause, and a proposed response is not proof that the response will work.

| Review element | What to record | How it helps |
|---|---|---|
| Decision path | Roles, events, pauses, and changes in direction | Shows where influence moved |
| Evidence | Customer actions, questions, requests, and refusals | Tests whether an explanation is grounded |
| Seller response | What was said, offered, delayed, or omitted | Exposes controllable choices |
| Next play | A warning signal, intervention point, and response language | Connects learning to future action |

The record should be short and specific. A concise review that changes behavior is more valuable than a long narrative that no one revisits.

### Bind the conclusion to the next action

A review becomes part of a system when its conclusion is attached directly to the next comparable opportunity. Do not stop at an archived meeting note. Create a short field memo for the next deal: identify the signal to watch, the role to engage earlier, the evidence to prepare, and the language to use when the same objection appears.

## A Practical Operating Method

Start with the next completed or stalled opportunity. Use a neutral facilitator when possible. Record observable facts first, then guide the team toward causes that can be changed.

Use these questions in order:

1. What did the customer do, and when did the decision path change?
2. What did our team believe at that moment, and what evidence supported that belief?
3. What could the team change in the next similar opportunity?

When the customer gives a broad reason, ask a grounded follow-up rather than accepting the first answer. If the customer says the price is high, ask how the value was assessed and where the perceived gap sits. Then ask whether a change in that area would receive personal support from the person speaking. This helps distinguish a general team objection from an individual's position.

During the review, prohibit personal blame. Replace statements about who failed with statements about which signal was missed, which assumption was untested, or which decision role was not engaged. A neutral process makes it easier for sellers to share uncomfortable evidence and supports a shift from defensive behavior toward learning behavior.

## What to Put in the System

A review system should make information usable, not merely searchable. For each recurring type of resistance, store the relevant customer language, the evidence supporting the interpretation, and the response options worth testing. Do not turn an example into a universal rule. Mark uncertainty where it remains.

The system should help a seller answer:

- Have we seen this objection before, and in what decision context?
- Which stakeholder role raised it, and who else supported or challenged it?
- What did the customer do after we answered it?
- Which evidence or question would have clarified the issue earlier?
- What should be prepared before the next meeting?

This discipline matters because a CRM is software, while system capability is a working method. A CRM can force sellers to fill in logs while leaving the information inert. A working method defines what is valuable to collect and how it will influence the next battle. It does not depend on a particular tool, but it allows any suitable tool to produce better value.

## Review Checklist

Use this checklist after a major opportunity is won, stalled, or lost:

- [ ] The final decision role and other influential roles are identified.
- [ ] The decision path is recorded as events, not only as a final outcome.
- [ ] Customer actions are separated from customer explanations.
- [ ] Seller assumptions are stated and linked to available evidence.
- [ ] The review avoids judgments about personal quality or intent.
- [ ] The unresolved barrier is described with appropriate uncertainty.
- [ ] Customer language and effective questions are preserved.
- [ ] The next comparable opportunity has a warning signal to monitor.
- [ ] An earlier intervention point and responsible role are identified.
- [ ] The conclusion is converted into a practical field memo.

## FAQ

### What is PSS5 system capability?

It is one dimension of a professional sales capability framework. It emphasizes that a sales organization should not depend only on individual heroes. It should build a repeatable, inspectable, and iterative way to collect information, move it through the team, and turn it into collective ability.

### How do we stop a review from becoming a blame session?

Separate responsibility from signal. Use a consistent template focused on customer behavior and the decision path. Record facts before discussing causes, and direct the discussion toward factors the team can change. A neutral facilitator can protect that boundary.

### How is system capability different from a CRM?

A CRM stores records. System capability defines which records matter and how they should affect future decisions. Without that method, activity logs may not improve judgment. With it, the CRM becomes a place where decision signals can circulate and remain useful.

### How do we get frontline sellers to invest in review quality?

Show the operational benefit. When managers use review insights to guide opportunity support or resource decisions, they should name the insight that informed the choice. Sellers can then see that careful evidence improves the chance of receiving useful support. Keep the record short and concrete.

### How does a team uncover the real reason behind a refusal?

Do not treat the first explanation as complete. Ask what the customer means by the objection, what part of the proposal creates the gap, and whether the speaker would support a change. Grounded follow-ups can distinguish value confusion, need misalignment, group resistance, and an individual position. Preserve uncertainty instead of forcing a story.
